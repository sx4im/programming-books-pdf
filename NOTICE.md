# Notice — copyright, indexing, and takedown

This repository is a **curated index of links and documentation**. It does not host PDF binary files in git.

## What we host

- Markdown language guides under `docs/`
- A web catalog (`web/data/books.json`) and UI under `web/`
- Links to resources (often public Google Drive shares maintained by the project owner, plus official documentation)

## What we do not claim

- We do **not** claim ownership of third-party book content, trademarks, or publisher materials.
- Book titles and linked materials remain the property of their respective authors and publishers.
- The MIT license applies to **this repository’s curation and code**, not to linked books.

## Compliance posture (keep the project healthy on GitHub)

To reduce the risk of repository removal or account action:

1. **Respond quickly to valid copyright notices** — remove or replace disputed links promptly.
2. **Prefer legitimate sources** — official docs, author-hosted free editions, and lawfully shared files.
3. **No placeholder / dump-site links** — validated by `web` scripts and contribution policy.
4. **Contributors suggest titles only** — maintainers attach Drive links; random unverified URLs are rejected.
5. **Educational framing** — this is a learning index, not a warehouse of unauthorized files.

GitHub may disable content when a valid DMCA notice is filed. Following this notice and removing disputed links quickly is the best way to keep the project available.

## Copyright / DMCA takedown requests

If you are a rights holder (or agent) and believe a link infringes your copyright:

1. Email the repository owner via the contact method on their [GitHub profile](https://github.com/sx4im), **or** use GitHub’s official DMCA process:  
   https://docs.github.com/en/site-policy/content-removal-policies/dmca-takedown-policy
2. Include:
   - Your contact information and authority to act
   - Identification of the copyrighted work
   - The exact repository path / book title / URL to remove
   - A statement made in good faith under applicable law

We aim to **remove or disable access to the disputed link within 48 hours** of a complete, good-faith notice (sooner when possible).
